A recruitment agency places candidates with client companies. The recruiter receives a job vacancy from a client company and screens the existing candidate database. If no suitable candidates are found, the recruiter posts the vacancy externally and waits for applications. Once applications are received, the recruiter shortlists candidates and submits profiles to the client. If suitable candidates were already in the database, the recruiter directly shortlists and submits profiles. The client reviews the submitted profiles and decides on next steps. If the client rejects all profiles, the recruiter is notified and begins a new search, repeating the process from the screening step. If the client selects one or more candidates for interview, the recruiter schedules the interviews. After the interviews, the client makes a hiring decision. If no candidate is selected, the recruiter is notified and begins a new search. If a candidate is selected, the recruiter issues a placement confirmation to both the client and the candidate, and the process ends.
