A hospital manages patient referrals between a general practitioner, a hospital intake department, and a specialist. The general practitioner examines the patient and decides whether a referral is needed. If no referral is needed, the general practitioner prescribes treatment directly and the process ends. If a referral is needed, the general practitioner sends a referral request to the hospital intake department. The intake coordinator reviews the referral and checks availability. If no specialist slot is available within the required timeframe, the intake coordinator notifies the general practitioner and the process ends. If a slot is available, the intake coordinator books the appointment and notifies both the general practitioner and the patient. The specialist receives the referral, examines the patient, and records a diagnosis. If follow-up treatment at the hospital is required, the specialist schedules a treatment plan and the process ends. If no further treatment is required, the specialist discharges the patient and notifies the general practitioner, after which the process ends.
