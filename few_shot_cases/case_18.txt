An insurance company processes claim submissions internally. When a new claim is received by the claims intake team, a claims handler is assigned and the claim is registered. The registered claim then triggers two parallel tracks: the fraud detection team runs an automated fraud check, and the assessment team reviews the supporting documents. Once both checks are complete, the claims handler evaluates the combined results. If the claim is flagged as fraudulent, the claims handler rejects the claim, notifies the customer, and the process ends. If the claim passes but documentation is insufficient, the claims handler requests additional documents from the customer, and once received, the assessment team re-reviews them and the results are evaluated again. If the claim is approved, the finance team processes the payment and the claims handler notifies the customer of the approval, after which the process ends.
