A government agency manages grant applications submitted by non-profit organisations.
The applicant organisation submits a grant application to the grants office.
The grants administrator registers the application and checks whether all required documents
(e.g. project proposal, financial statements, organisational registration) are included.
If the documentation is incomplete, the grants administrator notifies the applicant of the missing documents
and requests resubmission. The applicant collects the missing documents and resubmits them,
after which the grants administrator checks completeness again.
Once the documentation is confirmed complete, the grants office simultaneously forwards the application
to the technical review panel for a project feasibility assessment and to the finance department
for a budget adequacy review.
The technical reviewer assesses the project feasibility and records their verdict.
The finance officer reviews the proposed budget and records their verdict.
Once both verdicts are recorded, the grants administrator reviews the combined outcomes.
If either verdict is negative, the grants administrator sends a rejection notice to the applicant and the process ends.
If both verdicts are positive, the grants administrator issues a grant approval letter to the applicant and the process ends.
