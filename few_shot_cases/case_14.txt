A software company manages bug reports submitted by clients. 
The client submits a bug report to the support desk. 
The support desk reviews the report and determines its severity. 
If the bug is low severity, the support desk logs it in the backlog and notifies the client that it will be addressed in a future release. 
If the bug is high severity, the support desk escalates it to the development team. 
The development team investigates the bug and determines whether it can be reproduced. 
If the bug cannot be reproduced, the development team closes the report and the support desk notifies the client. 
If the bug can be reproduced, the development team deploys a fix and notifies the support desk. 
The support desk then confirms the resolution with the client. 
In all cases the process ends after the client is notified.
