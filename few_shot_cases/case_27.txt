A municipal authority manages building permit applications submitted by contractors.
The contractor submits a building permit application to the permits office.
The permits clerk registers the application and checks whether all required documentation
(e.g. architectural drawings, site plan, structural calculations) is included.
If the documentation is incomplete, the permits clerk notifies the contractor of the missing documents
and requests resubmission. The contractor collects the missing documents and resubmits them,
after which the permits clerk checks completeness again.
Once the documentation is confirmed complete, the permits office simultaneously forwards the application
to the urban planning department for a zoning compliance review and to the building safety department
for a structural safety assessment.
The urban planner reviews the application for zoning compliance and records their verdict.
The safety engineer assesses the structural safety and records their verdict.
Once both verdicts are recorded, the permits clerk reviews the combined outcomes.
If either verdict is negative, the permits clerk sends a permit refusal notice to the contractor and the process ends.
If both verdicts are positive, the permits clerk issues the building permit to the contractor and the process ends.
