A company manages employee onboarding for new hires submitted by the HR department. 
The HR officer submits a new hire request to the onboarding office. 
The onboarding coordinator registers the request and checks whether all required documents 
(e.g. signed contract, ID copy, tax forms) are included. If the documentation is incomplete, 
the onboarding coordinator notifies the HR officer of the missing documents. 
The HR officer collects the missing documents and resubmits them, after which the onboarding 
coordinator checks completeness again. Once the documentation is confirmed complete, 
the onboarding office simultaneously forwards the request to the IT department for system access 
setup and to the facilities department for workspace preparation. 
The IT department sets up the necessary system accounts and records their completion. 
The facilities department prepares the workspace and records their completion. 
Once both departments have recorded completion, the onboarding coordinator reviews the combined status. 
If either department has flagged an issue, the onboarding coordinator initiates a rework task to 
resolve the issue and then reviews the outcomes again. If both completions are confirmed, 
the onboarding coordinator sends a ready-to-start confirmation to the HR officer and the process ends.
