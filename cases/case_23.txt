A company manages invoice processing for invoices submitted by suppliers. 
The supplier submits an invoice to the accounts payable department. 
The accounts payable clerk registers the invoice and creates an invoice record. 
The clerk then checks whether the invoice matches the corresponding purchase order. 
If the invoice does not match, the clerk notifies the supplier of the discrepancy 
and the process ends for that submission. If the invoice matches, the accounts payable department 
simultaneously forwards the invoice record to the budget controller for budget verification 
and to the line manager for expenditure approval. The budget controller verifies whether 
sufficient budget is available and records their verdict on the invoice record. 
The line manager reviews the expenditure and records their verdict on the invoice record. 
Once both verdicts are recorded, the accounts payable clerk reviews the combined outcomes. 
If either verdict is negative, the clerk sends a rejection notice to the supplier and the process ends. 
If both verdicts are positive, the clerk processes the payment and sends a payment confirmation to the 
supplier and the process ends.
