A hospital manages patient referrals submitted by external general practitioners (GPs). The GP submits a referral request to the hospital's admissions office. The admissions clerk registers the referral and checks whether all required medical documentation is included. If the documentation is incomplete, the admissions clerk notifies the GP of the missing documents after which the process for the admission clerk and the GP for that referral. If the documentation is complete, the admissions office simultaneously forwards the referral to the specialist department for a medical assessment and to the insurance office for a coverage verification. The specialist conducts the medical assessment and records their verdict. The insurance officer verifies the patient's coverage and records their verdict. Once both verdicts are recorded, the verdicts are sent to the admissions clerk. Then the admission clerk reviews the combined outcomes. If either verdict is negative, the admissions clerk sends a rejection notice to the GP and the process ends. If both verdicts are positive, the admissions clerk sends a treatment approval to the GP and the process ends.
