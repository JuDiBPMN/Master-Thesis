A publishing house prepares a book for release. 
When a manuscript is accepted, 
the editorial manager kicks off the production process by simultaneously assigning the manuscript to the editor and to the layout designer. 
The editor proofreads and corrects the manuscript. 
The layout designer formats the pages and designs the cover. 
Once both the editing and the layout are complete, the print manager sends the files to the printer. 
The printer produces a proof copy. The editorial manager reviews the proof. 
If the proof is approved, the print manager places the full print order and the process ends.
 If the proof is rejected, the editorial manager identifies the issues and the layout designer applies the corrections, 
 after which a new proof is requested and the review repeats.
