A retailer manages product return requests submitted by customers.
The customer submits a return request to the customer service department.
The customer service agent registers the return request and checks whether the request includes
all required information (e.g. order number, proof of purchase, reason for return).
If the information is incomplete, the agent notifies the customer of the missing information
and the process ends for that submission.
If the information is complete, the customer service department simultaneously forwards the return request
to the warehouse team for a physical product inspection and to the finance department for a payment eligibility check.
The warehouse inspector examines the returned product and records their verdict.
The finance officer checks whether the transaction qualifies for a refund and records their verdict.
Once both verdicts are recorded, the customer service agent reviews the combined outcomes.
If either verdict is negative, the agent sends a return rejection notice to the customer and the process ends.
If both verdicts are positive, the agent approves the return, processes the refund, and sends a refund confirmation to the customer and the process ends.
