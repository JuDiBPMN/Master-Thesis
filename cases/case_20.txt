A city council manages building permit applications submitted by applicants. The applicant submits a permit application to the permits office. The permits officer registers the application and checks whether it is complete. If the application is incomplete, the permits officer notifies the applicant of the missing information and the process ends for that submission. If the application is complete, the permits office simultaneously forwards it to the urban planning department for a zoning compliance check and to the safety inspection department for a structural safety review. The urban planning department assesses zoning compliance and records its verdict. The safety inspection department conducts the structural review and records its verdict. Once both verdicts are recorded, the permits officer reviews the combined outcomes. If either verdict is negative, the permits officer issues a rejection notice to the applicant and the process ends. If both verdicts are positive, the permits officer issues the building permit to the applicant and the process ends.
